Split note: an 80/20 split keeps 65,660 emails for training and holds out 16,415 for testing. 20% is enough here because over 16,000 unseen emails give very stable metric estimates, while 80% leaves the models plenty to learn from. The split is stratified on the label, so the phishing share is 52.2% in training and 52.2% in testing - neither side is accidentally easier than the other. random_state=42 makes the split reproducible on every run.

Vectorizer decision: the probe scores are effectively tied (F1 0.9805 vs 0.9801, a gap of 0.0003). Since the measured difference is within noise, TF-IDF is chosen on principle: it down-weights words that appear in almost every email and emphasises distinctive vocabulary, which usually generalises better than raw counts, and it is the representation suggested in the project PDF. All Week 3 models will therefore train on TF-IDF features.
